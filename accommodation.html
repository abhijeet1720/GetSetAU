Finding Accommodation in Melbourne

Finding a safe and affordable place to live is one of the most important steps after arriving in Melbourne. Here are tips and resources to help you secure your ideal accommodation.

Types of Accommodation

  • On-Campus Housing: Convenient but limited spots; check your university's housing office.
  • Share Houses: Renting a room in a shared house or apartment with other students.
  • Private Rentals: Leasing your own apartment or studio, usually more expensive.
  • Homestays: Living with a local family, great for cultural immersion.

Best Suburbs for Students in Melbourne

Look for areas with easy transport to your campus, safety, and affordability.

  • Brunswick: Vibrant, multicultural, close to RMIT and Melbourne Uni.
  • Fitzroy: Artsy, great cafes, a bit pricier.
  • Hawthorn: Affordable with diverse food options, good train connections and close to Swinburne Uni.
  • St Kilda: Near the beach, lively nightlife, a popular student choice.
  • Carlton: Close to Melbourne Uni, packed with student-friendly facilities.

Tips to Avoid Rental Scams

  • Never pay any money before inspecting the property in person.
  • Use trusted websites like realestate.com.au and domain.com.au.
  • Verify the identity of the landlord or agent.
  • Be cautious of deals that sound too good to be true.
  • Always get a written rental agreement.

Useful Resources

  • Flatmates.com.au – Find share houses and roommates.
  • Homely.com.au – Property listings with neighborhood insights.
  • Student.com Melbourne – Student-specific accommodation.
  • Victorian Consumer Affairs – Renting rights and laws.
